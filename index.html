Improving the robustness of multi-modal deep learning systems

Typically, a multi-modal neural network exploits information from different channels and in different terms (e.g. images, text, sounds, sensor measures) in the hope that the information carried by each mode is complementary, in order to improve the predictions of the neural network. Nevertheless, in realistic situations, varying levels of perturbations can occur on the data of the modes, which may decrease the quality of the inference process. An additional difficulty is that these perturbations vary between the modes and on a sample-per-sample basis. The novel solution proposed in this thesis enables neural networks to focus dynamically on the best combination of modes, i.e. the modes containing the least amount of perturbations while having the highest intrinsic informativeness for the predictive task at hand. Promising results were obtained, as an example, the displayed graph shows how the performance (F1-score) evolves when increasing the amount of perturbations (NSR, noise-to-signal ratio) on one of the input modes. It can be observed that the performance of the neural network equipped with our method (blue line) remains stable, whereas the performance using standard data augmentation (red line) decreases drastically. A thorough presentation of the methodology and results can be found in the report.

Mapping unknown environments using multiple robots

The objective of this project was to build two robots from scratch, whose goal are to explore and map unknown environments as quickly as possible. More precisely, the robots are coupled with a program running on a computer, where the latter receives information messages coming from the robots. Those messages are then used by the program to construct a map of the environment in real-time and compute the next instruction for each robot. One of the main challenges was to induce a cooperative behaviour between the robots (e.g. collision avoidance, redundant work). The final system achieved a 30-40% speed-up on average, with respect to the mono-robotic exploration. A demonstration video of the result can be seen below.
